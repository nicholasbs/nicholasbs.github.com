Starting up

  • First, know thyself
    The most important thing for being an entrepreneur is to know yourself.
  • Looking for a cofounder? Reverse the order of operations
    Look for a cofounder before you seriously develop an idea, or at least keep yourself open to completely different ideas. Find someone you gel with, and then develop an idea together.
  • The next peak
    Doing a startup is like climbing a mountain. You usually only see the peak you're currently scaling, and it's rarely the summit.
  • Carcassonne and when to pivot
    If you're on a path that, even if you execute perfectly, you won't end up where you want to be, then you know it's time to pivot.
  • Starting a company is hard. So what?
    Startups face tons of obstacles, and that's what makes them fun.

Life and productivity hacks

  • How to ask for things
    You can maximize your odds of getting what you want by minimizing the work the other person has to do to help you.
  • Don't fool yourself
    One problem with having lots to do is you can trick yourself into always switching to the next thing without ever really doing anything.
  • How to hack your motivation function and stay motivated
    Have a narrative for your life.
  • Living in 80 square feet
    Constraints breed innovation.
  • Trading with friends for fun and profit
    Trade requires trust, but it also builds it.
  • Life optimization, or, brief thoughts on what has and has not worked in improving my day-to-day life
    Don't be afraid to experiment.

Writing and thinking

  • What if something you're certain of is wrong?
    If you want to think new thoughts, try this: Take something you think is true, and then assume it's false.
  • Can interview puzzles help find startup ideas?
    Their answers frequently lie in assumptions we don't even know we're making.
  • Thoughts on why writing is valuable and how to do it
    A lot of writing is bad because it's written in a way that nobody would actually speak.

Living life

  • What Sam Altman taught me about risk
    Nothing is ever as risky as you think it is.
  • Live without regrets
    When opportunities present themselves, take them, even if it's uncomfortable.
  • An open letter to my future self
    Humans tend to become less open to change.

Miscellaneous

  • The value of symmetric social connections decays
    The network that figures out how to scale into the mainstream without sacrificing the value of its symmetric connections is going to be insanely valuable.
  • Another advantage of specialized apps
    One of the reasons I started using Foursquare and Instagram is that their specialization means I can feel less narcissistic.
  • Networking shouldn't be a dirty word
    Think of the five best connected people you know. Are they jerks?
  • Happy Birthday, Macintosh!
    Thanks for all the memories.
